So you want to start longboarding?

Here are a few helpful hints

1. Picking a Board for Longboarding

Shorter boards will allow you to turn sharper and are more agile, but they don't offer much stability and as you increase your speed the board often begins to wobble. while a longer board offers that stability for increased speed it is difficult to get sharp turns.

2. Picking Your Wheels

There are a few more decisions to make before you can ride your board, we will start with wheels

If you you are looking strictly for speed than the larger wheels will be great for you, however they do have a tendency to have slower acceleration. Also wheel hardness makes a huge difference to the performance of the board. While the wheels will last longer if they are hard, they will be less stable and rough surfaces can be difficult to ride on.

3. Get Your Other Gear Together

While you may not like the look of protective gear, it will really help especially as you are learning to ride. There are a few certainties associated with longboarding but one of them is that you will fall, having the right gear will help make sure you can get back up and try again.
